  4. How does enrollment vary between an introduction to law class and an introduction to criminal law class?
    Interestingly enough, there seems to be far greater interest in criminal law classes than in standard introduction to law classes. The enrollment numbers for criminal law J7 and political science 71A reflect this trend. One theory is that high profile cases such as the Menendez murder trial, the Rodney King case and the O.J. Simpson murder trial have generated an increased interest in areas of study involving criminal law. With the mass media placing such a high priority on criminal law cases, it is no wonder why students are enrolling in criminal law classes over general law classes by a six to one margin.

  5. How do the services of the Department of Justice differ from the Federal Bureau of Investigation?
    While both agencies come under the authority of the executive branch, the Federal Bureau of Investigation is actually a division of the Department of Justice. When the Department of Justice learns of a criminal or civil wrongdoing, the Federal Bureau of Investigation investigates the matter further. Once a prosecutorial decision is made, the Department of Justice assumes responsibility for the matter and an attorney is assigned. Therefore, Attorney General Janet Reno technically has authority over the F.B.I. However, it is well known that in sensitive issues, the Director of the F.B.I. reports directly to the President of the United States.
