Desiging Technologies for Managing Migraine

 

Advisor: Prof. Yunan Chen

 

Migraine presents with a heterogeneous set of symptoms: some people only have hours of occasional and mild attacks, like dizziness and pulsing pain; others may suffer more frequent attacks with severe pain like vomiting and physical disability for up to a week. Although migraine is not associated with severe debilitating consequences, it impacts people’s normal day-to-day lives significantly. Many patients are unable to move during migraine attacks, and have to wait until the symptoms disappear to resume their normal activities.

 

Since the physiological basis of migraine is unknown, patients often face many challenges in understanding their own disease and in maintaining their normal lives during migraine attacks. We conducted qualitative interview study aimed at understanding how people manage their migraine in daily lives and discovering opportunities for technology to support patients’ needs. Our preliminary finding reveals that migraine management includes not only individual work but also collaborative work within the patient’s social circles.
