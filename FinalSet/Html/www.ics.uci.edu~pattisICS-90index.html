Course Goals and Plans

The three main goals of ICS 90 are to help new students make a successful
transition to UCI, to build a sense of community in ICS, and to introduce
new undergrads to the school, some of the faculty, the majors we offer,
and the work we do.

In a typical class session, two faculty members will speak for half an hour
each about their work and background; Rich and David will also spend a
half hour addressing more general issues about college life, the field of
computing, and strategies for success.

Student Expectations

ICS 90 is a one-unit course graded Pass/Not Pass. We have designed it
not to require much attention outside of the class meetings, but we do
expect every student to attend every class. (If an emergency comes
up, send email to Rich Pattis as soon as possible).

It's in class where the learning happens, so you should be present.

For issues that come up between class meetings, we have set up a forum at
piazza.com. Sign yourself up and then participate by posting questions or
supplying answers. We may also have opportunities to use Piazza in class
for polls and surveys. You may bring your smartphone or laptop for this
purpose (but think carefully before using it for other purposes).

Seating

Please arrive as soon as possible and take your assigned seat quickly and
quietly. If you do not sign the attendance sheets when they are passed out,
please come up to the front of the room after class to sign it. With 300
students packed into a classroom, we need to minimize the ambient noise:
please put away all laptops and smart-phones for the entire class and pay
attention to the speakers.
