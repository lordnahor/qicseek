INTRODUCTION

In today's business market with it's high level of competition for survival many firms will do anything to gain an edge over others in the world. In order to save and make money firms will tend to cut corners many different ways. Here are just a few ways a business may cut corners:

  • Using lower quality goods for the production of their own goods.
  • Using a substitute product by diluting their own good.
  • Trick consumers, for example by false advertising.
As we can see, cutting corners is not that hard to do. With today's technology it becomes even easier for a business to try and gain the extra edge. With the emergence of computer advancements such as the Internet and the World Wide Web. It provides businesses with a "new tool".

How businesses use the "new tool" to advertise on the web.

This emergence of the "new tool" allows businesses to advertise their business, their products, and general information. Advertising on the web is not hidden. It takes only a few minutes for a user on a web browser to find examples of business advertising. Businesses tend to advertise two different ways: directly or indirectly.

Direct advertising by a business is when a firm will put out a specific page on the web showing their product, a specific service they provide via the web, and even products for sale through the web or the phone. One can find many examples of direct advertising, such as:

  • Ordering pizza from Pizza Hut
    •
  • Visit a mall with that carries almost anything you could want.
    •
  • Have lobster FedExed from Nova Scotia.
    •
These are just to name a few of the many, many types of advertising found on the web. For example one can just look up a catalog of vendors or just "surf the net" for a product.

Indirect advertising by a business is very commonly found, whether the firm knows they are advertising or not. They are advertising. This is the whole idea of what indirect advertising is all about. Indirect advertising can be found through magazines and general information provided by businesses. Here are a few examples of indirect advertising:

  • Entertainment Weekly gives us a special subscription offer.
    •
  • AT&T gives a list of 1-800 phone numbers.

Like direct advertising, indirect advertising can be found almost everywhere on the web. The question now is: How does a firm advertise? A business can do this two ways. The first way is to make a company home page and the second way is to go through an advertising service on the web. Even home pages can be processed and published through special services. Services like Business Cards Online, Internet Design Group, and Zynet Services can provide firms ads and home pages for the right price. A good example of a business home page is Apple Computer's home page or Sony Music's home page.

How advertising with the "new tool" will change the way we will buy, sell, and do business.

The internet has vastly changed the way that businesses have traditionally bought, sold, advertised, and communicated, between themselves and with customers. The internet has increased the speed in which businesses can reach the consumers. It has reduced the costassociated with advertising. The internet expands the range to which advertising and marketing reaches. Communication between the consumers and suppliers has also been bettered through the use of the internet.

The cost of advertising and marketing a product has traditionally been high and to change the product line and then communicate the changes to the consumer can be very cost-prohibitive, especially if done frequently. Assuming a 250 page catalog costs on the average $1.55 to produce, mailing this to 250,000 people costs $387.800! The internet, even if we assumed comparable cost can reach 20 million people. Think about the costs to update and send out a new catalog for summer. A list of virtual catalogs can already be found on the internet. An advertisement on the internet can be constantly updated and changed for a low cost. The speed of internet marketing, sales, and service can also help to keep ahead of the competitor. Consumer trends can almost immediately be addressed and changes made to change the firm's actions accordingly. This saves the company time and money.

The range and scope to which internet marketing and advertising reaches is another change with which business must cope. larger and more diverse groups of people can be reached on the web that may greatly enhance a firm's exposure and help them to reach a new consumer base that would normally be restricted due to cost or lack of information. The global aspect of the internet also makes any firm international and provides customers all over the world. Quite a benefit to any business.

The aspect of increased and better communication between the producer and consumer may be one of the most beneficial. Not only can a consumer order over his own computer at home or at the office, the firm can process his order and even communicate via e-mail. E-mail is the most used of all internet tools and could become an important part of the business world. Examine a scenario where a business provides the marketing, and sale of a product over the internet. A person across the world orders it and instantly his order is processed. If he chooses he provides his e-mail address and fills out a short questionaire about himself and the product that he has just purchased. The firm also provides an e-mail address if the consumer has any problems. A week late the product has arrived and The consumer tries to put it together or install it or figure out how it works. He cannot. He e-mails the company which has 24 hour help and they send on-line text, pictures, diagrams and anything else that the consumer may need. This also is coupled with the standard 1-800 phone line. So the buyer has his product, all the information he can ask for, plus he gets it in the fastest time possible. The business saves time and money on paperwork, gets info about the consumer that can help in their research and marketing, plus they have a fully satisfied customer. The better knowledge gained by the company about the consumer through better communication results in the elimination of costs by offering what the consumer does want. The information and feedback can also result in the bettering of a product. This may be more likely through the internet due to the impersonal nature of using a computer to write your comments via a comment form, or answer some questions, ratherthan talking to a representative on the phone or having to write and mail a questionaire.

While all of this may sound as a too-good-to-be-true advertising utopia, be careful of what really exists and what actually can be done of the internet. While 16 million people use the internet and its potential seems limitless, The actuality is that advertising here is a little bit different than the television-based kind that the average American is used to. New graphics, audio and video are beginning to transform the internet, the text based approach is still used heavily. Information on the internet is demanded and given. The evolution has provided a new look to the internet, but a certain web etiquette has been required of those that use this resource. The result is a give and take idea. Considerable quantities of information are given and passed on the web and is given freely. The company that wishes to take and not give back may not be received so openly among the internet users.

Is the "new tool" good or bad for buisness?

CONCLUSION-Our opinion

The internet is a tool that can be very useful for business. As both the internet and the business world evolve, the union between the two is inevitable. Already firms are discovering the usefulness and potential that the internet brings to their businesses. But can the internet handle the increased influx of use and information that can be provided by the swarm of businesses and their marketing and advertising strategies. Under its full potential the internet would be a wonderful thing. The convenience that would be provided to consumers is without equal in our society today. Firms would better be able to serve, communicate, and expand across this electronic highway. Business would be transacted in such a new, faster way that would involve more interaction and information retrieval that would cut down on the costs normally associated with business today. The internet, however, is not ready today for this amazing influx of information. The development of the internet must equal the increased demand for the use on it. The increased use of business on the internet is not a bad thing, and in time it will develop into a great resource for businesses, but for now business should not overshadow the real purpose of the internet which is to provide information on a global scale.
