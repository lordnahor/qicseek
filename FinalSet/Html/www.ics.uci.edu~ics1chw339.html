  1. My roomate is talking about doing a designer drug called "ecstacy" for the first time with some of her "friends". I want her to be informed about the substance; So, where can I find a personal account of the effects and overall experiences related to the drug?

    On the Web Crawler Home Page, type in "DRUGS" for a list of references related to the topic of drugs. On that list is an extensively researched and extremely informative piece of work called "E is for Ecstasy" by Nicholas Saunders. Everything on would ever need to know about the drug ecstacy (MDMA), is detailed in this document. Tis includes a history of the substance, explanations of what it is, what it does, how it works, where it comes from, who takes it and why, where to get it, and legal issues involved. Among the information in this report is an indepth description of the authors personal experiences of taking ecstacy, as well as experiences of friends and aquaintances. There are also suggestions for first time users, which give tips on how to ensure a safe and optimally satisfying first time experience.

  3. I'll be traveling around the nation and the world this summer. I really like to attend rave parties, where they play House and Techno music. Since it is hard to find these parties, and you ususally have to know someone to find out where the good ones are, how can I find out where to go for these parties while I'm on the road?
    The "RAVE" scene has it's own listing on the world wide web! This seems amazing, since most of these parties are illegal. In order to find out where the party is in any part of the U.S. or many parts of Eastern Europe, just use the     Global Rave Information Database. From there, you can select any region where Raves are happening, including San Francisco, L.A., Chicago, New York, the U.K., and Germany. After selecting the desired region, you can click on "view" and you will get a list of all the up and comming rave parties in the area. So, no matter where you are, you will be able to find out where a rave is, as long as you can access the world wide web.

  4. I am enrolled in a Physics class which is a study of the earth's atmosphere. For my term report, I want to include some kind of picture of the earth, with emphasis on the atmosphere. Where can I find such a picture?
    On the     Silicon Graphics Home Page, there are a wide variety of graphics resources to choose from. There are a couple of interesting pictures of planet Earth from space. One of them is a very intense picture of the globed in infrared, which shows the global climate zones. You can also find many other satalite photos of the Earth's atmosphere, including pictures of CO2 concentrations around the globe, and where UV rays are highest on the Nonproliferation and International Security division Home Page. These web site should prove to be useful and informative for your Physics class.
