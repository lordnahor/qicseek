  1. What does the word 'wacipi' mean in Sioux?
    In Sioux wacipi literally means 'dance' and can be defined as a powwow, a cultural gathering where American Indians can dance, socialize, and just have a good time. Usually a traditional wacipi will occur during the summer months and can last about three days, beginning at noon and continuing on till the wee hours of the morning. There are various styles of dance for men and women, each with their own origin myths. The men may dance Traditonal, Grass Dance, or Fancy War, whereas the women dance Traditional, Fancy Shawl, or Jingle Dress. For each style, the regalia clearly defines the differences. However; one thing that is common to the dancers outfit is the footgear, also known as moccasins which can be adorned with quillwork or beadwork.

  3. What tribe did the U.S. Government first promise physician services as partial payment for rights & property ceded?
    The U.S. Government's first Federal-Tribal relationship that promised physician services was with the Winnebago tribe in 1832. Today this agreement is referred to as the Indian Health Service (IHS). The IHS was established to meet the health needs of American Indian and Alaska Native people on and off the reservations. The services provided include hospitals, emergency rooms, and health management. Even though this modern-day medical service is provided as a result from past treaties and agreements, the American Indian people are not always openly accepting towards a 'strange' medicine. For hundreds of years, they have relied upon traditional means of curing - their spirituality (healing the whole, rather than the part) and turning to Mother Earth for roots and herbs.

  4. Where would one go to learn about Labanotation?
    Interestingly enough one can learn about Labanotation here at UCI. There are currently two professors within the Dance Department who are certified Labanotators: the Chair of the Dance Department - Professor James Penrod, and Mary Corey. Labanotation is the way in which one records dance movement and steps through a system of symbols that represent body parts, direction (three-dimensionally) and timing (phrasing and musicality). All the elements involved with a dancer and her movements. This method of notation allows choreographers to preserve their 'works' in exact duplication, rather than relying on memory or handwritten notes (usually the interpretation gets lost with words and is not always comprehensible by others reading the notes). It's comparable to learning a foreign language.

  5. What can the World Wide Web offer?
    It has become a cold-hard fact that computers are an integral part of today's society. The World Wide Web was proposed by Tim Berners-Lee of the CERN laboratory in Geneva, Switzerland to be a system which could easily be accessed universally by anyone with Internet connections. By means of the Web, one has connections to various forms of multimedia - sound, music, video, games and CD-ROM. Since the Web was set up to be as simple as possible, a person can locate information such as universities in the United States, find out the weather in a certain city or even read cartoons. The search is endless and 'surfing' the Web allows people in various fields a chance to locate their interests and needs.
