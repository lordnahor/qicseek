  1. Introduction

    What is the first image of Japan? You would say a small country, expensive to live, a great economic power, etc. The last characteristics of Japan is deeply related to Japanese education. The academic achievement of Japanese students is pretty high. For example, in 1986 94 % of students who graduated from lower-secondary school entered upper-secondary school. That is pretty similar to that of the United States. 35 % of them continued on to University, which is lower than that of the United States but higher than that of west European countries(bib.1). This high ratio of Japanese education has helped Japan develop so much, but at the same time she has many problems concerning education.

  2. General Information

    Educational system in Japan is basically 6-3-3-4; 6 years of elementary school, 3 years of each lower and upper secondary schools, and usually 4 years of college. Some people go to 2 year junior college where 90% of students are women. And there are many other institutions. Special training schools which offer students special skills, such as "computer science" and "vocational training", and Miscellaneous schools where they can learn various kinds of subjects(bib.2).

    Education in Japan is free and compulsory from the 1st grade through the 9th grade. Schools go by trimester system, and students are supposed to attend school for at least 210 days. School year usually begins in April and ends in March. They have to go to school Monday through Friday, and one-half day on Saturdays. Right now they are considering if they take a day off on Saturday or not in public shcool(bib.1,bib.2).

    Private schools started playing an important role in education especially in upper-secondary schools. Most of the elementary schools are virtually public school. Private elementary schools are less than 1%. However, when it comes to lower and upper secondary schools, the ratio goes up to 5% for lower-secondary school and 24% for all upper-secondary schools. There is a tendency that private schools take care of students in terms of studying a lot more than public schools(bib.2).

  3. Problems of Japanese Education System--college entrance examinations

    3.1.Memorization

    First of all, one of the biggest issues of Japanese education is college entrance exams. That drives everybody crazy especially last year in upper-secondary school. Because of that they have to spend much time on studying useless things. A key word for studying is "memorization" which won't stay on their mind for a long, and which doesn't help them acquire anything important. In the case of history ,for instance, they have to memorize all the year when something important happened. that is it. They don't go into depth for that. They just cover the surface of history(bib.2).

    3.2.Limited Subjects

    Since subjects which are tested in the exams are decided in advance, most of the students only study those subjects instead of their intersets. There is definitely lack of choices and little effort is made for that. That is seriously killing various personalities for each person(bib.1, bib.2).

    3.3.Poor English

    Although Japanese students spend a lot of time on studying English, most of them can't use it practically. In lower and upper secondary school they focus on mostly grammar and readings which occupy most of the part on English exams. They study English only for that. That is why there are so many Japanese who can't communicate in English. Besides, there are few teachers who can speak English well at school, and basically they are lacking in native English speakers(bib.2, bib.3).

    3.4.Cram School="Yobiko"

    This kind of college entrance examinations increase the number of students who go to cram schools called "Yobiko" which help students prepare for entrance exams. So basically many students go to both regular school and Yobiko just to get in a college. Actually Yobico is more famous for those who didn't enter any college previous year. They are called "Ronin", and to be a Ronin at least for a year is very common especially among men. Ronin occupy about 40% of college entrance, which is very large amount. That shows how competitive the college entrance is. There are some who spend more than 5 years as Ronin, which seems like real waste of time. Those kind of people are dying for one vary famous university, most of the case, University of Tokyo(bib.2).

    3.5.Connection to job

    The reason why so many people care about particular Universities is because it has much to do with tehir future jobs. There is a very close linkage between University background and employment. If they want to work for big 1st-rank company, the most important things for them is not what they studied in college but the fact that they graduated from certain prestigious University. Especially private sector doesn't expect University graduates to learn something special at college. They would rather train them by their own way, and make them fit in their own sector(bib.1).

    3.6.Big Influence from Future Job

    For the final purpose to get a good job, competition of college entrance exams became keen and harsh. That aslo affects education system in both lower and upper secondary schools. Nowadays even elementary schools have been influenced by that, and they are forced to study for getting in good school which has good education system for entering 1st-rank colleges(bib.2).

    3.7.Japanese Students from Overseas and Foreign Students

    In this stressful situation, another problem is coming up. That is the case for those who spent many years outside of Japan. When they come back to Japan, they will have some problems to fit in school especially in lower and upper secondary school. Unless they go to oversea Japanese school, they will be far behind in the class, and it is so hard for them to get in college through regular examinations. Since they are lacking in English-speak teachers, and many of the students are studying for entering good schools, it would be difficult for them to follow the school system in Japan. Thus, many of them tend to go to either American school or International school(bib.5).

  4. Conclusion

    In order to solve those problems, some reforms have been conducted. Through those reforms the National Council on Education Reform introduce some new proposals. First of all, internationalization is an issue for every education level, especially for higher education. Life long education which allows adult people to have education is also a principal proposal, such as expansion of University entrance opportunities. There is a need for improvement of teaching foreign languages, particularly English. The government invited many native English speakers to improve spoken English for Japanese. The most controversial issue here is the harsh college entrance examinations. The ministry urged Universities to have more substitute for the exams, such as admission by recomendation, admission by special skills, admission for those who returned from overseas and who want to keep studying years after they graduated. If the system of the exams changes, there will be more selection of subjects which they can study instead of memorization, and acceptance for foreign students will become easier, too. Even though there is little evidence for that so far, hopefully those changes have been happening step by step(bib1, bib2, bib4, bib5).
