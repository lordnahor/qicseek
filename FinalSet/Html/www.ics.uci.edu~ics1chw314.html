  • About Hypertext...

     I must begin this with my personal definition and understanding of Hypertext. On entering ICS 1c I did not have a clue about what hypertext was. Hypertext,I have learned, is the way that normal people are allowed to use computers. What I mean by that is hypertext covers up all the messy computer commands that most people do not understand (like me before I took this course). Now when someone wants to write something on the computer, one needs to use all those messy commands (plain text). When people see what you have written they are looking at it in HTML (hypertext markup language)i.e., the pretty,clean stuff that we all see on Mosaic and the Internet. Now a link is merely how one makes those blue words that you click on that can send you from one document to another document. Please click on the highlighted markers in this article to get much more detailed information and directions on how to create your very own HTML document with links!

  • The weather...

     While looking for the weather in my favorite ski spot I happened to find several different weather reporting services. Curious about the weather in Florida? At this site you may view the entire state of Florida and then click on hi-lited cities for a current weather report. For weather all over the world all you need to do is hop onto the Weather Information Superhighway and choose just about anywhere in the world. Some sites include colorful satellite maps. You can then see for yourself how the weather system is acting and possibly predict your own weather forecast. My favorite so far is the local Oregon weather reports. Here you can hit the second `this' by local forecast and link into the University of Michigan's Weather Underground and receive information about many different cities in the U.S. Happy weathering!
