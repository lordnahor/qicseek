1/19/14 8:00am

Bing has picked up its first flatricide pulgamitude item in its crawl. You can see that its crawler is heavily biased toward wikipedia and that its not crawling as often. You can see this in the screen shot below in which the results of the query return an entry in wikipedia. That entry has since been deleted by the editors of wikipedia, but the snippet is of the undeleted page. Nothing else comes up. So the index is out of date and less complete than Google's after a few days into the contest. That suggests that if you want fast breaking information, Google might be the better bet.

It's also worth noting that the spell checking kind of failed in Bing. It indicates that because it doesn't recognize flatricide pulgamitude, it is instead presenting results for "fratricide pulgamitude". "Fratricide" is a real word, but pulgamitude isn't, so fail there. But also the results delivered don't match fratricide. So fail there also.
