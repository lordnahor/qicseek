ABSTRACT

Science and technological advancements made today appear or are begotten at an explosively increasing rate. The rate at which new technology develops now is highest in history. The modern computer and telecommunications infrastructure produced and gained from this increased rate has also afforded us with the revolutionary speed in which we hear and adopt the new technology. This all has indeed been affecting society in tremendously different ways---jobs have arisen, growth and relay of information efficiently increased, and increased national gross to name a few ways.

However the ability and potential to take advantage of our cutting edge technology rests not on how much of the new knowledge or technology we can produce but on society's ability and capacity to accept, absorb, and use it. What this means is that the limits of science and technological advancement as well as economic growth may be ascribed to society's ability to assimilate the new knowledge and transform it into a part of our lives.

The concern and belief that many within the political, scientific, and economic arena have regards then the approach that we must also develop new methods and approaches to broaden the capacity, increase the reasoning, and offset the limitations of our society. What follows in this paper is a discussion of a few areas in which significant efforts have been made as so the technology we have may become more useful and accessible to society.

This paper's principle intent is to provide attention to these areas and by no means an is argument for these areas. However, it is hoped after seeing the new perspective, that readers will be swayed over into adopting the belief for these areas of research otherwise known as Human Computer Interaction or HCI.

INTRODUCTION

I begin this discussion first defining what HCI is or what does it stand for. Human Computer Interaction is the study of the interaction between human beings and computers (Booth, An Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction, Lawrence Erlbaum, 1989, p.4).

In that article where Booth defines HCI, Booth also details the 5 more definitions of different areas within HCI:

Research into International Hardware and Software
Developing, studying and providing guidelines for various ways or interacting with computers, both at the software and hardware level
Research into Machine Models
Study how users interact with computers
Research at the Task Level
Determining how well systems meet user's needs
Research into Design and Development
Studying designers and design process
Research into Organizational Impact
Studying the impact of new systems on organizations, groups, and individuals
The also available HCI FAQ references Human Computer Interaction as :
  • "a body of knowledge about human limitations, human abilities, and other human characteristics that are relevant to design"
  • "the application of human factors information to the design of tools, machines, systems, tasks, jobs, and environments for safe, comfortable, and effective human use"
  • "a discipline concerned with the design, evaluation and implementation of interacting computing systems for human use and with the study of major phenomena surrounding them."---ACM SIGCHI Curricula for Human-Computer Interaction, 1992, p. 5

User Interfaces

Does the colors on the screen play a role is ease of use?
Yes. People's eyes are sensitive to different colors and different light intensities. Research (Taptafaporn and Saito 1990) has shown that display polarity and lighting conditions may be the causes of some users' eyestrain. It is recommended that the degree of both factors be dependent on the working environment and the individual. What is not recommended is reading from a monitor with a dark background, which causes pupil dilation (as in a dark room environment) and thus causes the image on the retina to defocus. The research concluded that having a positive polarity display, black letters on a white background, is physiologically and psychologically superior to the reverse.

Why don't developers just produce a comprehensively intense manual that provides all the answers?
Such a tome would be rather lengthy and often unmanageable for all individuals to carry about. In addition, each individual sees and experiences things in such a diverse manner, that it is nearly impossible to cover all the questions everyone has. Another trend is that it may be impractical since most users tend to not read the manual and refer to it as the last option. As the Golden Rules of the order of what to do when a user experiences difficulty with a system, many users follow this ordered list:

  1. Experiment for themselves.
  2. Ask the nearest person.
  3. Ask the office expert.
  4. Phone expert.
  5. Try online help.
  6. Look in the manual.

What's the big deal over having a modal dialogue box as opposed to a modeless one?
Modal ones are used when a user is required to first make the appropriate response to the dialogue box in order to continue. In many cases, this is not essential or not intuitive for a user to respond directly with the dialogue box. Making a modeless ones is preferred to allow a user to react in his or her own idiosyncratic way rather than get flustered figuring out what exactly needs to be done.

Is there a single easy in-lab way to determine if the operating system works synchronously in tuned with a user?
No. In order to test if it behaves the way a user expects it to behave is to allow numerous users to use it extensively. This is also known as beta testing.

What type of issues are involved when making an icon? It doesn't seem like a big deal.
When crafting icons, association relationships must be highly regarded. Many assume that the shape of an icon conveys enough meaning; this however is not true. Icons should be tested after creation to check for weak relationships. Tests have also shown that icons with names labeled on them make learning easier.

CONCLUSIONS

There are multiple issues involved in the interactions between humans and machines. Whether they be obvious or complex and subtle, when improperly dealt with, the issues restrain effective and efficient communication between a user and a machine. The small subset of issues examined in this document for just one area, user interfaces, may have seemed anal retentitive or trivial; but in reality, they are major issues being dealt with and researched continuously. Can there be one universal answer to all the issues involved? Most likely no. As our society continues to diversify itself in new and more innovative directions, our issues become more complex and unsolvable. However, through various approaches we can alleviate the complexity through HCI research. HCI research concentrates on three key elements:
  1. New interaction paradigms that enable users to communicate with computers and with others using computers in more effective, human ways;
  2. Fundamental understanding of the nature of interaction that will enable users to achieve their goals more directly, including a better understanding of the limitations of human information processing;
  3. Engineering methods for interactive system design and development that will improve the quality and reduce the costs of user interfaces for new systems.
What we need now is a call for the all the innovators and leaders in development to adhere them closely.
