  1. How is E-mail sent and received over the Internet?

    The Internet is now the fastest way to send mail from one place to another. In order to send and receive electronic mail, a person first needs a mailing address. This address is comprised of a name followed by a domain. The name is a selected identifier which is unique to each person, just like the first line of a regular mailing address. The domain serves as the computerized street address. It lets the "cyberspace mailman" know which computer system to deliver the mail to. Again, like a conventional mailing address, it is possible to have two people with the same name on the Internet, but they have different domains to distinguish between the two. This way, everyone on the Internet's mailing list has a unique and identifiable address. In order to find someone's e-mail address, just dial 411 (Directory Services) for help.

  2. How is information linked together on the World Wide Web?

    One of the greatest features of the World Wide Web is its ability to link documents and resources to each other. This connection of information is made possible by Hypertext Markup Language (HTML). The HTML Developer's JumpStation contains many resources on developing, editing, and converting HTML documents that can link to other documents on the Internet. There are also sites on the Internet such as The Web of Wonder which are specifically designed to provide over 1,500 links to places on the Internet.

  3. What methods of policing and control are utilized in regards to the World Wide Web and who is responsible for this task?

    With so many people accessing "cyberspace" at one time, security is an essential part of the Internet's success. CERN , the Switzerland based laboratory where the World Wide Web was developed, assumes much of the responsibility of policing the Web.CERN offers security through SHEN: A Security Scheme for the World Wide Web The purpose of SHEN is to develop way to secure mail, file transfers, and information in the Web. In addition, there are many services such as Security Resources and Internet Security Firewalls that focus primarily on preventing hackers from invading your computer. A comprehensive guidebook on Internet Security shows the many security issues facing Net users and how they can deal with these issues.

  4. How does one find information and resources in the World Wide Web?

    Due to the incredible number of documents, files, and pages accessible on the World Wide Web, it is often difficult to find out exactly where these sites are located. Thankfully, Search Engines are available which help to locate specific areas that exist in the Web. There are many types of search tools, or spiders, available that will help you find your way around cyberspace. The World Wide Web Worm is a common search tool that conducts searches by keyword, title, or URL. You can also search the web with The Whole Internet Catalog which offers an updated list of sites arranged by category. These search engines are the best way to find the exciting and interesting resources, services, and information that the World Wide Web has to offer.

  5. What is the United States' current economic position?

    The U.S. economy is facing tough times with the dollar slipping in value compared to other national currencies. Political unrest in Europe and other parts of the world have hurt U.S. foreign trade, while NAFTA and GATT have made efforts to increase trade revenues. While Bill Clinton's economic plan aims to "increase public investment" and "reduce the deficit", the recent Republican takeover of Washington has threatened this agenda. As a result, the inflation rate is threatening to rise and consumer spending is declining.
