  • What is HTML, and where can I learn how to create an HTML document ?

     Ahhhh, the excitement of HTML. HTML, or hypertext markup language provides structure to documents on the World Wide Web. HTML uses a series of codes to transmit data, which are in turn translated by a client (such as Mosaic, Lynx, or Viola, for example) into recognizable screen formats. The most valuable aspect of HTML lies in its inherently interactive nature; one can "click" on highlighted text with the aid of a favorite rodent, connecting to to new links, maps, images, headings, etc. in order to continue a certain search path or to veer onto another search course. HTML is still inferior to most desktop publishing programs, with constraints on font selection and graphics placement, and many limitations on relatively simple functions. For a good introduction to HTML, try the Beginners Guide to HTML, which covers the basic elements of creating an HTML document. Next you may want to reference Composing Good HTML, which can help with troubleshooting and improper use of various commands.

  • What do the South Georgia and Sandwich Islands have in common with Montserrat? Formerly "Where can I get my daily Tarot and I Ching readings on line?"

    South Georgia, the South Sandwich Islands, and Montserrat are all islands administered by the U.K. Montserrat is located in the Carribean, about 400 miles southeast of Puerto Rico, while the South Georgian and Sandwich islands nestle in near Argentina. Argentina claims that the nearby islands belong to them, but Brittain maintains control. Montserrat has an indigenous population of about 12,701, while South Georgia is populated only by the folks in a small military garrison; the South Sandwich islands are uninhabited. All are subject to volcanic activity, all count fishing as an important natural resource, and all are under Chief of State Queen Elizabeth II.
