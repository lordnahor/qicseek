  1. Who are the champions in wrestling?

     Well, that would depend on what type of champions you're talking about. Pro wrestling is a pretty diverse "sport" (ha ha), and as a result, is infamous for having more champions than challengers. Basically, there are two main federations nowadays. The biggest (and most profitable) is the WWF, or the World Wrestling Federation. The WWF has a history dating back to the 1960's where it has crowned several World Champions! You can even see a list of these monsters of mayhem! Interestingly enough, the WWF has what it calls an "Intercontinental Title" as well. This holder of this title has always served as the number one contender to the World Champ. There's also a comprehensive list of all the WWF's Intercontinental Champs. The second most-profitable wrestling organization is the WCW, or World Championship Wrestling. This one is owned by the world's "most stable" billionaire extraordinaire, Ted Turner. Like the WWF, the WCW has a wide variety of champions. However, the WCW saw its beginnings in the early part of the 20th Century. You can see this long list of WCW World Champions. The list of United States Title Holders (which is really the equivalent to the WWF Intercontinental Championship) can be found as well.

  2. Who are on the legislative committees in the South Dakota state legislature?

     The answer to this question depends on what branch of the legislature you mean. In South Dakota (as in most states), the legislature is bicameral. This means there are two separate "houses"--the state senate and the state assembly. Both houses have a number of special committees, which help fulfill their ultimate goal in getting legislation passed through the legislature. Such committees deal with South Dakota issues on both the state and local levels. You can see a listing of the members, the political parties they belong to, and the committee names of either the South Dakota House Standing Committees or the South Dakota Senate Standing Committees.

  3. What are the functions of the US State Department and Department of Health and Human Services?

     Let's start with the State Department. Basically, the State Department is the "eyes and ears" of America's foreign policy. Among other things, it sets out to establish relations with the rest of the world. There are several areas which the State Department handles. You can read more about these State Department roles. The Department of Health and Human Services wants to keep all of America well. In a nutshell, this wing of the Executive Branch is responsible for overseeing a wide variety of programs dealing with the health and well-being of all segments of America. You can find out more about the Department of Health and Human Services.

  4. What is HTML, and how does it work?

     HTML stands for "Hyper Text Markup Language." Simply put, HTML is a language by which one can go about creating documents to be viewed on a browser (such as Mosaic or Netscape). HTML helps make it possible for plain text to be "marked up". As John December and Neil Randall say in "The World Wide Web Unleashed", the current version of HTML is 2.0. While this version allows more features (such as graphics), the two note that it still has a ways to go. For instance, they say, HTML 2.0 is not yet equipped to do some specific, useful functions (such as desktop publishing). The two also note that HTML allows for "links" to other sites on the web. Thus, instead of being limited to a single web page, one can click on a link (if there is one on that page) and receive more information on the subject. HTML, they say, has made this possible. There is much more information available on what HTML is. If you were interested in finding out ways of programming in HTML, you could do that as well.

  5. What is new on Mosaic?

     In order to tell you what's new on Mosaic, perhaps it's best to describe what Mosaic is. Mosaic is a browser which allows a user to view a document/file in HTML format. Before Mosaic, information could only be presented in text form. With Mosaic, however, someone can look at accompanying images. Unfortunately, Mosaic is unable to display certain types of files currently (it needs the help of an add-on program) In addition, there are other limitations with Mosaic (one of which is its inability to incorporate other internet functions). Other than these limitations, Mosaic has proven to be a beneficial way of displaying information. The program operates on a few different terminal types. For our purposes, we can see how Mosaic operates on X terminals. Mosaic is also constantly updated. You can find out what's new on Mosaic. If you get stuck, you can always get help on how to use Mosaic.
