Otoe-Missouria � Where do you come from?
The name Otoe (OH-toe) was historically derived from Wat'odatan (wat-OH-dah-tah)" The story is about an otoe man and woman;they wanted to copulate", a
story joke given to them by related Missouri and Ioway.
They lived near the Iowa during early history between 1690 � 1700. The Oto, the Missouri, the Ooway, and Winnebago showed up in the prehistoric record as the Oneota culture. The Oto lived near the Iowa during both tribes earliest histories, the Oto being especially connected to the Blue Earth and
southwestern Iowa. After leaving Iowa by the 1750�s traveling along the Arkansas River and later crossing the Missouri River to Nebraska.
The Missouri lived along the stretch of the Missouri from its junction with their Mississippi to Southwestern Iowa. The French explorers named them. After the wars between the Sauk and Mesquakies during 1798, much of their land and diminished resources led them to almost distinction, The members that were left disbanded and sought refuge among the Oto.
Living in villages along the Platte River, the Otoe and Missouri still retained their hunting rights in Western Iowa. After ceding their lands in Nebraska in 1854, the Oto and Missouri were assigned the Big Blue River Reservation (Big Blue) on the Kansas and Nebraska border. During that period of 1880 the Indian Agency allowed the white farmers to buy the lands without the Oto-Missouria' permission. With the help of a few good
men from the Department of Interior, a small part of the money for that land was given back. With this they bought their own reservation in Oklahoma which was subsequently allotted; most of this land passing out of their hands. Now classified as one tribe , they are called the Otoe-Missouria of Oklahoma, near Red Rock, Oklahoma.
