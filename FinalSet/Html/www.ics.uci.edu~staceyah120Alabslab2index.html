Part 1: Hands-On Simulations

Background

The American television show Let's Make a Deal was hosted for many years by Monty Hall. The Monty Hall problem is a probability puzzle, loosely based on this game show and named after its original host. The problem became famous as a question from a reader's letter quoted in Marilyn vos Savant's "Ask Marilyn" column in Parade magazine in 1990:

Suppose you're on a game show, and you're given the choice of three doors: Behind one door is a car; behind the others, goats. You pick a door, say No. 1, and the host, who knows what's behind the doors, opens another door, say No. 3, which has a goat. He then says to you, "Do you want to pick door No. 2?" Is it to your advantage to switch your choice?

What do you think? Should you switch doors or not? Why?

Instructions

You are going to simulate the game show using a deck of cards which will be provided to you. Form a group of 6 people and divide your team into two groups of 3 each. In each group of 3 choose:
    1. The contestant
    2. The game show host (Monty Hall)
    3. The recorder
When finished with 10 rounds of the game, rotate roles and repeat the process so each person fulfills each role once. Therefore, you will complete 3 sets of 10 rounds one for each person as the contestant.

One round of the game goes as follows:
  1. Contestant: Turn around and close your eyes so you can't see what's happening.
  2. Host: Take 3 cards from the deck - one face card (J, Q, or K) and two non-face cards (A, 2,..., 10). The face card will represent the car, and the two non-face cards the goats. Place the three cards face down on the table in a random order, keeping track of which card is the face card. Make sure the cards are aligned the same way so the contestant does not see any difference between the three cards.
  3. Contestant: Turn around to face the table and guess which card hides the car (the face card) by pointing to it. It will not be revealed yet!
  4. Host: You are going to reveal one of the un-chosen cards to show the contestant that it is not the car (the face card). Here is how you decide what to reveal:
    • If the contestant guessed a non-face card (goat), reveal the REMAINING NON-FACE CARD.
    • If the contestant guessed the face card (car), RANDOMLY choose which other card to reveal. Do NOT let the contestant see you hesitating, or he/she will know that they guessed the correct card. They must not know if they guessed the correct card or not at this point.
  5. Contestant: You now have a choice. You can KEEP your original guess, or you can SWITCH to the other remaining card. If you choose the face card, you win a car! Use your intuition each time to decide whether to keep your original guess or switch to the other remaining card.
  6. Recorder: Keep track of the combination of whether the contestant switched or not and won or lost.
After finishing the hands-on simulations, use your data (combined with your classmates' data) to make a guess as to what you think the probability of winning the car is under each of the two strategies (switching or not).
