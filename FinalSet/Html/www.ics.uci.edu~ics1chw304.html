  1. What is a protocol?

     A protocol is an agreement on how different computers will communicate or exchange information. An example would be HTTP which is commonly used by the World Wide Web. Other kinds of protocols include Ymodem, Xmodem, Kermit, Zmodem, etc. Zmodem is the most common one used today because of its special ability to continue transmitting a particular file after it has been interrupted (also known as "crash-recovery"). Many different kinds of protocols are being introduced in order to improve the efficiency and reliability of transmitting files.

  2. What is an HTML?

     HTML stand for HyerText Markup Language. It is a specific SGML_DTD (Standard Generalized Markup Language_Document Type Definitions). It is a specific kind of code in order to make documents more readable (and more attractive) to the reader. In fact, this document you are reading was written in HTML. It is the primary markup language used by the World Wide Web. The language of HTML has become more complex as new versions come out. Consumers are demanding new features constantly. So far, there are three levels of HTML.

  3. What is a democracy?

     In the world of politics, democracy is a form of government in which the citizens of the entity decide over public policies. It is sometimes known as the 'majority rule'. Most contemporary Western countries nowadays are using democracy as a form of government. In order for the citizen to have an influence over public policy matter, they must must participate in some way. The purpose of democracy is for the people of the society to have a say over how the government is run. It is the opposite of a dictatorship or a totaltarian country.

  4. What is political participation?

     Political Participation refers to the actions a citizen of a society can take in order to influence the process of the government. Political participation is most common with democratic societies. There are many different kinds of political participation. Voting in elections is the most common and direct way of influencing the government. Other forms of political participation include campaigning, protests, donations, attending a political meeting, contact locally with others, etc. However, with all these kinds of participation available to the citizens, political participation has gone down in some contemporary democrarcy countries especially the United States.

  5. What formats are picture files stored in?

     There are many ways in which pictures are stored in a file. The most popular ones today are GIFs and JPEGs which are most commonly used in networking and bulletin board services. The important concept of a picture format is compression and access time in order to display the picture. Other kinds of picture format include BMP (Windows), PCX (PC Paintbrush), WPG (WordPerfect), MSP (Microsoft Paint), EPS (PostScript), MAC (MacPaint), etc. MPEG is the most common format used today to display animations of pictures. Different kinds of format will be developed in the future to increase speed and compression.
