  1. What is the World Wide Web and how will I be able to access it?
    According to Tim Berners-Lee's article, "The World-Wide Web," the World-Wide Web (W3) was developed to be a pool of human knowledge, which would allow collaborations in remote sites to share their ideas and all aspects of a common project." And according to December and Randall, "The World Wide Web is a convergence of computational concepts for presenting and linking information dispersed accross the Internet in an easily accessible way." So, how can I find out more about the WWW? Well, there's The World Wide Web Unleashed by December and Randall, World-Wide Web Home, and the Educational Online Sources.

  2. What are some of the special connections within the Internet?
    First lets define What is the Internet? It is the cooperatively-run, globally-distributed collection of computer networks that exchange information via the TCP/IP protocol suite. The Internet consists of many internetworked networks, called internets (with a small i). An internet is a single network that uses the TCP/IP protocol suite, and some internets are not connected to the Internet. There are some Special Internet Connections which contains information on games, business, science, computers, recipes, and much more.
