  2. What is SGML and what does it have to do with HTML? SGML, or Standard Generalized Markup Language, is a general language used for text information processing. It was adopted for this purpose as an international standard, by the name ISO 8879. Its underlying goal is to facilitate efficient distribution and retrieval of information in a generalized language that will not mandate a specific appearance or interpretation by the client. The SGML standard actually defines the markup language used in creating and disseminating hypertext documents in Hypertext Markup Language, or HTML. HTML defines the structure of a hypertext document such that any client can interpret it according to its program's capabilities. Thus, HTML takes the basic philosophy of generating a flexible markup language and applies it to hypertext and multimedia systems such as the World Wide Web.

  5. Where can I find out more about Extropians and better yet, what/who in the world is it? The best place to find out more about Extropians is to go directly to the source, or rather to the document on the Web entitled Frequently Asked Questions on Extropians. Extropianism is actually more of a mindset rather than a platform of concrete and hard held ideas. They are most interested in exploration of life and its many facets with a particular emphasis on technology as a means of bridging political and social differences. Extropians, ultimately then, or perpetual and self sustained optimistic explorers who tend not to hold any particular allegiances while advocating infinite creativity and total and boundless expansion. This is not to say there are no Extropian principles to be unearthed in such a morass of "liberation theology". Extropians are intimately tied to humanism in its philisophical base(feeling that the "supernatural" and/or whatever is considered "outside" the range of human knowledge or power is ultimately a pointless and backward unit of study), but attempt to transcend the limits of humanism. Thus, they term themselves as "transhumanists", they believe that we can transcend physical limitations of our biology, culture and environment through the wise and creative use of science and technology.
