  1. As a political science major (and an ardent Democrat) with an emphasis in public policy issues on a national level, where can I find any information on Bill Clinton and what conservatives in the nation think about what the administration is currently doing?
    My answer is a subscription to the outspoken publication: The Washington Weekly. Unfortunately, this publication can only be read after a costly payment of $7.95 for 20 issues. Nevertheless, The Washington Weekly is available for all interested readers through Internet on Gopher (dolphin.gulf.net, port 70) and the World-Wide Web (http://dolphin.gulf.net/). The CONTENTS of the publication are available for public use at no cost. Besides this source of political information from the conservatives of this country, one can also find a more factual account of White House ongoings through White House Watch.

  2. What are the names of all the UCI faculty within the political science department, and how can I contact them on e-mail?
    The names of all the UCI political science faculty members are always available in the UCI catalogs in hard copy. For those that would rather sift through the Web to acquire the names of faculty, their areas of emphasis, e-mail addresses, and possible pictures, the Political Science Department has a Home Page just for those interests! Some of the more well-known faculty members include Professor James Danziger, former Chair of the Department of Political Science and current Chair of the Academic Senate; Professor David Easton who is credited for developing the basic jargon associated with the study of poltical science; and Professor Mark Petracca who has always remained in the public eye with countless columns in newspapers as well as television appearances. For more general information on the department, including sources on the undergraduate and graduate programs, Click here.

  3. I will be travelling to Russia this summer. Where can I get information on Russian culture including some examples of Russian literature, art, and obscenities?
    To become more familiar with some Russsian culture, there are three sources that would provide interesting and humorous information. To become more familiar with political literature on the current debates ensuing in Russia, one can scan the contents of Slavic Review. Some articles discuss topics such as national identity in Russia, parliamentary elections, and book reviews. Beyond current social and political issues within the country, one can become familiar with the culture by scanning the Internet Art Galleries which include an Electronic PhotoGallery featuring the work of Russsian artists such as Alexander Kholopov and Boris Mikhailov. Lastly, to genuinely familiarize oneself to the country, some working knowledge of Russian obscenties is almost a must!

  4. I wish I had actually read Vannevar Bush so that I would have done a bit better on Quiz #1. Unfortunately, I lost the hard copy of the article, but I am just aching to read all about him. Where can I find his article?
    The article, "As We May Think," written by Vannevar Bush is located within the References section of the ICS 1C Home Pages. His article has been used to cite the origin of artificial intelligence and multimedia. Bush brings to the public eye in this brief work the notion that in some respects computers can be made to become very similar to the human mind. His article discusses the nature within which information is disseminated and how this information can be made accessible through computer organization. Other references within the course are also available.

  5. Where can I find a sample document in HTML format to give me a good idea as to how to create my own document?
    The most useful HTML Sample is within the Reference Section in the ICS Home Pages. This reference outlines in basic terms the meaning of Hypertext Markup Language and the basic structure of an HTML document. There are eight HTML References available in the HomePages including NCSA's Beginner's Guide to HTML and James Powell's Introduction to HTML. In these documents, one can find information on acronym expansion, creating HTML documents, basic markup language, additional markup language, structuring titles, headings, and paragraphs.
