Martin Shelton is a third-year PhD student at UC Irvine's Department of Informatics at the Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences. He does research on social behaviors related to digital tools, particularly in social websites, gaming, and scientific computing.

Martin is currently working with Dr. Judy Olson on a longitudinal study to examine how students and faculty use Google Apps in university settings. He is also working with Dr. Bonnie Nardi on a project to unpack the influence of networked media websites on external conversations. His most recent work is focused on building and user testing social recommendation and rating systems for scientific articles.

Martin has worked previously at Twitter as a user research intern with Liz Ferrall-Nunge. He is interested in user experience, particularly for social and media-sharing applications.
