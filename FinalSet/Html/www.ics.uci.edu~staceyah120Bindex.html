Books on R
Two popular introductory books on R are:

  • A Beginner's Guide to R by Alain F. Zuur, Elena N. Ieno, and Erik Meesters; Springer, 2009.
  • Introductory Statistics with R by Peter Dalgaard; Springer, 2008.

These two books can be downloaded for free if you are on the UCI campus network from http://link.springer.com/. There are many other books written on R, and a simple search will produce many results. In particular, Springer has a book series of "Use R" books which you can explore here.

R Studio
RStudio is a nice user interface for R, and many of my previous students prefer using RStudio over the basic R. It is still command based, but has some nice features like, e.g., keeps track of your plots, easily shows command history, lists objects in the workspace, etc. You can download RStudio here. You must already have R downloaded and installed on your computer.
