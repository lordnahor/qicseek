  1. What is the relationship between a client and a server?

    Clients and servers are agents, or programs, that work for a purpose--acting on behalf of something. A client is the software that allows users to access and browse the World Wide Web. Clients can display HTML pages in their desired format. They are refered to as browsers and respond to a user. Servers, on the other hand, are computers on which the Web documents reside and which runs HTTP software to permit Web transactions. Look into the various client software products and World-Wide Web server software products available for more information.

  2. What is HTML and what is its purpose?

     HTML stands for Hypertext Markup Language. One uses HTML to create a Web page. Web clients display HTML pages according to their coded format. HTML is a protocol designed specifically for the Web: any browser can read and display a written document that fits its needs, for html carefully defines the document for this purpose. The reason why html can be so versatile for browsers is that it expresses information in raw form, without consideration for formatting. For more information on html, go to the HTML Document Tutorial.

  3. I want to apply to graduate school in English at the University of Washington. What are the admissions requirements?

     The University of Washington Home Page is a good place to start on information about the university. There are photographs, descriptions about the university in specific, and about the surrounding environment. The University of Washington also includes graduate school entrance policies. A student must have a strong GPA (averaging a 3.00 or above) and should have high scores on both the general and English subject GRE tests. Also, the student should be able to display consistency in having completed her undergraduate program.   4. I want to do an independent study with a professor in the cognitive sciences department who is working with linguistics. Who does research in this field?

     A couple of professors in the school of social sciences do research in linguistics. A good way to start one's search is by getting a broad view on who the professors in the cognitive science department are. In this search, one can also find descriptions of each professor's research areas. The two professors who conduct research in linguistics are as follows: professor Barbara Landau, who works with spatial knowledge and language learning, and professor Mary Louise Kean, who works with linguistic theory and biological foundations of higher mental processes.
