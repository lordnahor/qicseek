ICS 143A ‒ Summer Session  

Course Contents:

  • This course are available via the Distance Learning Center web site

Textbook:

  • L. Bic, A. Shaw, Operating Systems Principles, Prentice-Hall, 2003 (ISBN: 0130266116)

Course Structure:

  • Lectures:

This is an online course. Each lecture is a video recording using a PowerPoint presentation. The length varies based on the topic, ranging from 5 to about 30 minutes. Copies of the PowerPoint slides are available on the course web page. The lectures are based on selected sections of the book. You will need headphones or speakers to follow the lectures..

  • Homework:

Each week`s lectures are accompanied by a set of homework assignments to practice the presented material. All homework assignments need to be turned in by the due date.

o   HW 1 is due on Wednesday of each week before midnight

o   HW 2 is due on Saturday of each week before midnight

(No credit can be given after the solutions have been posted.) The homework will not be graded but solutions will be posted after the due date.

Please note: The points earned for homework make up only a relatively small percentage of the total grade and are intended only as an incentive for you to complete and turn in the work on time. However, the problems on the weekly quizzes and the final exam will be very similar to those on the homework. Thus doing the homework is essential for doing well on the quizzes/exam.

  • Quizzes:

There will be a quiz every week, which must be taken during a specific time window. Each quiz will cover the material of the current week.

o   Time window: Sunday of each week, 7:00pm-9:00pm (if you want to have the full hour then you need to start before 8:00pm)

  • Final Exam:

There will be a final exam at the end of the course. This will be given on the UCI campus (not on line). It will be comprehensive, covering all the material of the course.

o   Date/time/location: Tuesday September 10, 7:00-8:50 pm, DBH 1200 (Donald Bren Hall)

 

Grading:

  • Grade composition:
    • Homework: 10%
    • Quizzes 40%
    • Final 50%
  • Exams:
    • The quiz with the lowest grade will automatically be dropped.
    • The final exam is comprehensive and is an "open book/open notes" exam
    • All quiz/exam problems will be similar to the homework problems.
  • Grading will be done on a curve as follows: Approximately:
    • 25% of students will receive A+/-
    • 25% of students will receive B+/-
    • 30% of students will receive C+/-
    • 15% of students will receive D+/-
