What We Do
Buffalo Cafe is a full-service Indian Taco catering company with one simple specialty, making indian tacos or buffalo meat dishes for those special occasions for a virtual Native American Palate Experience. At Buffalo Cafe, when we say "full service catering," we mean exactly that. You can call on us for virtually any event. From quickly setting up the kind of indian style buffet of buffalo dishes, "the Indian Taco" and herbal teas, fresh-squeezed juices, and assorted variety of quality Indian Fry breads with unforgettable sweet confection toppings that can get your meeting off to a delicious start, to planning and executing the NativeAmerican Palate Experience party that involves an authentic cultural aroma and ambviance, sit-down dinner for hundreds of people, Buffalo Cafe can handle it all. So you can clinch that vital sales contract, or finally get to dance with that long-lost relative at the family reunion, secure in the knowledge that all of the Native American Palate Experience of culturally tasteful details for your important occasion will be traditionaly and meticulously tended to.

Who We Are
A success since the early 1900's when the Relocation of Indian Tribes were put on Reservation Camps. The Indian Taco came about when the troops gave the indians provisions as part of the treaty agreements. Buffalo Cafe is the brainchild of a native american couple - Cheryl Blackhawk -who both met at a non-profit federally funded Native American program and enjoyed setting Indian Taco booths to help raise funds for the program. Many people all over the nation who has been to a Native American event and tasted the Taco always comes back for more. The urge to eat hot, tasty fry bread stays with them until the next event. In 1996, continued food booths at fairs, powwow, and native american cultural presentations and discussions about their interests and about life goals reunited the two, who determined to create a catering company that went well beyond the ordinary. The final touch that allowed their goal to become a reality was provided by a chance encounter with an investor who enjoyed the food and wanted place with service to share with his friends, family and colleagues this same Native American Palate Experience he enjoyed. This silent partner - fresh out of MBA school, dreaming of a business that she could back wholeheartedly, and still enchanted by his Native American Experience of the same foods by the Buffalo Cafe.
